Kitafarms Theory of Change Map

From Inputs to Equitable Green Economy "INNIVATING FARMING AND GRAINS DISTRIBUTIN IN AFRICA" (Target: 200 Cooperatives)

1. NEEDS IDENTIFICATION (The Problem)

CLIMATE VULNERABILITY
  • Drought, Flooding, Intermittent Rainfall
  • Threatens 100% of harvest
  • Soil Degradation & Reduced Fertility
  • 50% lost of crop to drought and 100% to flooding
ECONOMIC EXCLUSION
  • No Market Access / Exploitative Middlemen
  • Suppresses Income that Traps Farmers in Debt during harvest
  • Lack of Access to Irrigation & Quality Inputs
  • extreme poverty and hunger
SOCIAL KINKS
  • Farmer-Herder Conflict
  • High Labor Burden on Women (manual Irrigation)
  • Youth Unemployment and Migration
⇩

2. INPUTS (Resources Invested)

PHYSICAL AND FINANCIAL:
  • Investment Capital ($200,000 USD)
  • Solar-Powered Irrigation Systems (400 units)
  • Quality Seeds and Farm Tools
  • market access and linkage
HUMAN AND SOCIAL:
  • Training and hands on site (Climate-Smart, Agroecology)
  • Youth/Women Mobilization and Land Agreements
  • Engagement with Village Heads/LGA
⇩

3. OUTPUTS (Direct Results of Activities)

ORGANIZATION AND ASSETS
  • 400 F-Kit Cooperatives formed (4,000 farmers).
  • 400 Solar Pumps installed and operational.
  • Central Hub established for buy-back.
  • 1000+ hectares cultivation (climate smart)
  • Market linkages secured with institutional buyers.
GENDER INTEGRATION
  • Women-Led Clusters established.
  • Women receive direct digital payment training.
  • Youth receive Solar Technician/DFA training.
CLIMATE ACTION INITIATED
  • Agroecology Protocols (No Burning of crop waste) established.
  • AI Climate Forecasts distributed leveraging our e-commerce platform
  • Land allocated and secured.
  • all year round farming
⇩

4. INTERMEDIATE OUTCOMES (12-18 Months)

CLIMATE AND ECONOMIC SHIFT:
  • Successful Dry-Season cultivation and Harvests (100% flood risk elimination).
  • Farmers achieve 3X increase in household income.
  • Circular Farming commerce established.
  • 50% reduction in crop lost due to drought during rainy season
SOCIAL AND GOVERNANCE SHIFT:
  • Women gain direct digital income control.
  • Youth employed as certified technicians.
  • Immediate reduction in farmer-herder conflict incidents.
CRITICAL ASSUMPTIONS:
  • A1 (High Confidence): Farmers can adopt/maintain solar tech and uphold quality standards.
  • A2 (High Confidence): Institutional buyers will honour Guaranteed Buy-Back Contracts.
  • A3 (Medium Confidence): Crop farmers and herders will sustain the commercial circular farming relationship.
⇩

continous economic activities and growth among local in rural farming communities and companies in urban area
